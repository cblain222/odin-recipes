Pizza

A neapolitan pizza

This simple recipe will make you wonder why you ever got takeout pizza.

Ingredients

For the dough

  • 4 cups of plain flour
  • 2 teaspoons table salt
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 1/2 cups of water
  • Cooking spray or olive oil

    • For the sauce

  • 1 can of San Marzano tomatoes

    • For the toppings

  • 12 ounces of fresh mozzarela, torn into small chunks
  • 2 ounces of basil leaves
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil

Steps

    Make the dough:

  1. Combine the flour, salt, and yeast in the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with a dough hook attachment. Mix to combine.
  2. Add water and mix to combine.
  3. Knead the dough on low speed for 10 minutes. The mixture should come together into a unified mass that barely sticks to the bottom of the bowl as it kneads. If the dough is sticking, add more flour, 1 tablespoon at a time, to the mixer while it is running, until the mass barely sticks to the bowl. If the mixture is too dry, add water, 1 tablespoon at a time, to the running mixer.
  4. Cover the bowl tightly with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 8 hours and up to 72 hours.
  5. When ready to make the pizza, transfer the dough to a floured work surface. Divide the pizza dough into 4 even sections.
  6. Coat 4 small containers or bowls with nonstick cooking spray or olive oil.
  7. With floured hands, form each section into a ball.
  8. Place 1 dough ball into each coated container or bowl. Lightly spray the top of each dough ball with nonstick cooking spray or coat with olive oil.
  9. Cover each container or bowl tightly with plastic wrap, and let rise at room temperature for 2 hours. Each dough ball should double in volume.

    10. Make the sauce

  10. Make the sauce by either pushing the tomatoes through a food mill or simply pulsing them in a food processor until a chunky liquid is formed.

    11. Assemble the pizza

  11. Heat the oven to at least 475 F, higher if your oven allows. If using a pizza stone, place it in the oven to heat as well.
  12. Onto a well-floured surface, stretch out each ball of dough into a 10-inch circle. Use a floured rolling pin if you need to. Donâ€™t worry about the dough being a perfect circle.
  13. Lightly flour a pizza peel making sure to cover where the dough will sit. Place one rolled-out piece of dough onto the peel and shake gently to ensure that the dough is not sticking.
  14. Spread about 1/3 cup of sauce over the dough, leaving about a 1/2-inch of the edge bare.
  15. Top the sauce with 1/4 of the mozzarella slices. Tear a few basil leaves and place them on top (according to your liking), and drizzle with 1 tablespoon of the olive oil. You can also add the basil and olive oil after the pizza is cooked.
  16. Transfer the pizza from the peel to the hot stone by gently shaking the peel back and forth until the pizza slides completely onto the stone.
  17. Bake about 5 to 7 minutes, until the cheese is bubbly and the crust is charred in places.
  18. Repeat with the remaining dough balls and ingredients.
